How to Create a Locked Folder in iPhone Photos App (iOS 17+)

If you hand your unlocked iPhone to a friend to show them one picture, they are only a few swipes away from seeing your entire camera roll. Apple offers built-in tools to prevent this, and independent security apps provide even stronger vaults. Here is exactly how to restrict access to your private media.

By Editorial Team • Updated Recently • 12 min read
Quick Answer

To create a locked folder using native iOS 16/17/18 features: Open the Photos app, select the images you want to hide, tap the three-dot menu (or share button), and choose Hide. To ensure this folder requires Face ID or Touch ID to open, go to your iPhone's main Settings > Photos, and toggle on Use Face ID. Your hidden items are now locked in a dedicated album at the bottom of the Albums tab.

What You Need to Know Before Starting

Securing pictures on an Apple device comes down to a fundamental choice: relying on the operating system's built-in barriers, or moving the files into an entirely separate, encrypted application. The native route is fast and deeply integrated, but it leaves your media subject to the same passcode that unlocks your phone. A third-party vault requires an extra step to save media, but establishes a completely independent security perimeter.

Before proceeding, verify your operating system. Apple drastically changed how photo privacy works in recent years. If you are running iOS 15 or older, the "Hidden" album simply moves photos out of the main timeline—it does not actually lock them behind biometrics. True Face ID locking was introduced in iOS 16 and refined in iOS 17 and 18.

METHODS

How to Create a Locked Folder in iPhone Photos (iOS 17/18)

Method 1: The Built-in Hidden Album Easy

Apple's native solution is the "Hidden" album. By default, moving a picture here removes it from your library view. To make it a true locked folder, you must enforce biometric authentication in your settings.

  1. Open the Settings app on your iPhone.
  2. Scroll down and tap on Photos.
  3. Ensure the toggle for Use Face ID (or Touch ID on older models) is turned ON.
  4. Open the Photos app and select the images you wish to conceal.
  5. Tap the circle with three dots in the corner (or the share icon depending on your exact version) and select Hide.
  6. Confirm you want to hide the items.

To view them later: Tap the "Albums" tab, scroll to the very bottom under "Utilities," and tap "Hidden." Your phone will scan your face before displaying the contents.

Limitations: If your Face ID fails repeatedly, the phone falls back to your standard device passcode. Anyone who knows your lock screen PIN can access this folder. Furthermore, these photos remain part of your iCloud Photos backup.

Method 2: Lock a Folder in Files on iPhone Moderate

Many users ask, "Can I lock a folder in Files on iPhone?" The answer is nuanced. You cannot lock a standard folder directly in the iOS Files app with a password. However, you can create a password-protected document (like a Note or a ZIP file) that acts as a secure container.

  1. Open the Notes app and create a new note.
  2. Tap the camera icon and choose Choose Photo or Video to import your sensitive media into the note.
  3. Once imported, tap the three-dot menu at the top right and select Lock.
  4. Set a specific password for this note (or choose to use your device passcode/Face ID).
  5. Go back to the Photos app and delete the original pictures (and clear them from "Recently Deleted").

Limitations: Storing high-resolution videos in the Notes app can quickly degrade performance and consume excessive storage. This method is best for a handful of static images, not a large media library.

Troubleshooting & Owner Recovery

If you are struggling to manage your secure files or have lost access, consult these verified owner-recovery steps.

How to recover photos from a locked app

If you have forgotten your dedicated PIN for a third-party vault, the recovery process depends strictly on whether you established backup credentials during setup. Security tools are designed to resist bypass attempts. You must check your email for a license key or recovery code, or use the "Lost Registration" portal provided by the developer. Uninstalling the app will not bypass the lock and will likely result in the deletion of your hidden files.

App lock behavior after phone restart

A frequent query is how security holds up during system reboots. Native iOS Face ID requires your numeric passcode immediately following a restart before biometric scanning resumes. Similarly, high-quality independent vaults remain sealed upon reboot and will demand their master password before launching into memory. They do not fail open.

Can I access a locked folder from another device?

If you are utilizing the native Apple Hidden album, you can view it on an iPad or Mac logged into the exact same Apple ID, provided iCloud Photos is syncing. If you use a third-party vault, you can only access the files if the app features cloud synchronization (like the up-to 5 device sync in premium tiers) and you log into the service on the secondary hardware.

Can you create locked photo albums on iPhone?

Yes. Beginning fully in iOS 16, Apple transformed the standard "Hidden" album into a biometric-locked space. You cannot create multiple distinct locked albums natively; all hidden media goes into a single repository requiring Face ID or Touch ID to view.

Can I create a folder for pictures and lock it on iPhone apart from the hidden folder?

Not natively within the Photos app. If you require multiple categorized secure folders (e.g., "Financial Receipts" and "Personal Media"), you must download a third-party security application that supports custom folder architecture.

What happens if I delete the locked folder on iPhone?

You cannot delete the native "Hidden" folder itself, but you can turn off its visibility in Settings. If you delete photos from within the hidden folder, they move to the "Recently Deleted" folder, which is also protected by Face ID on modern iOS versions, before being permanently erased after 30 days.

Can someone bypass app lock by restarting the phone?

No. Both native Apple security (Face ID) and properly engineered third-party applications remain fully engaged through a hard reset. In fact, restarting forces the requirement of a manual passcode entry, disabling biometric convenience temporarily for enhanced security.

Can I lock a notes folder on iPhone?

Yes. The Apple Notes app allows you to lock individual notes, but not an entire folder at once. You must swipe left on a specific note and tap the lock icon to secure its contents behind a password.

How does fingerprint app locking work on older devices?

On iPhones equipped with Home buttons (like the SE), Touch ID functions identically to Face ID for the purpose of securing the hidden album. The hardware securely enclave processes the fingerprint data and passes an approval token to the software without exposing the biometric data itself.

Is the iPhone locked photo folder encrypted?

When your iPhone is locked, the entire file system is heavily encrypted. However, when the device is unlocked and you are just opening the Hidden album, the lock acts more as an access control barrier rather than a separate layer of file-level encryption. For distinct file encryption, dedicated software is necessary.

SUMMARY

The Bottom Line

If you simply want to keep a photo off your main timeline when showing a friend a meme, Apple's native Face ID hidden album is sufficient. However, if you are storing financial records, highly personal media, or need a security layer that doesn't rely on your phone's main lock screen PIN, migrating to an isolated vault is the correct protocol.
